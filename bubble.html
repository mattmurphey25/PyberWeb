Bubble Chart

The bubble chart compares four different variables at the same time. The x and y axes are straight-forward showing number of riders in the city vs. average fare in that city. The next two variables are the city type and the number of drivers in the city. The numbers of drivers in the city is shown by the size of the bubble, while the city type is shown by the color. As expected, the number of rider, number of drivers, and average fare numbers line up pretty well based on the notion that more riders increase the number of drivers, and this in turn lowers the prices. The city type shows, also as expected, that higher populations will yield more riders.
